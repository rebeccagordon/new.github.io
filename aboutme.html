About me



I was born and raised in the Bay Area, California. After graduating with a bachelor's from UC Santa Cruz, I moved to Spain to travel, research ADHD, and learn Spanish. I returned to the US in 2015, and started career in addiction and HIV research at Brown University School of Public Health. I continuted research at the Center for Alcohol and Addiction Studies while completing my Master of Science in Behavioral and Social Health Sciences.

During the 2020 pandemic I started working at Yale School of Medicine in the Emergency Department at Yale New Haven Hospital where I conducted a clinicaial for hyperkalemia management. I also conducted studies related to recovery after undergoing cardiac surgery. I am now pursuing my PhD in a field that combines public health and developmental psychology perspectives.
